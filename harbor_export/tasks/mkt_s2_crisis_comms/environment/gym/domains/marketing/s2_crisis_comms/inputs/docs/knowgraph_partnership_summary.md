# KnowGraph Education Partnership — Internal Summary

**Classification:** Internal — Confidential
**Last updated:** 2026-02-15
**Owner:** Priyanka Shah, VP Product Marketing

## Partnership Overview

**Partner:** KnowGraph Education, Inc.
**Founded:** 2021 (San Mateo, CA)
**CEO:** Dr. Anand Mehta (former Pearson digital product lead)
**Funding:** Series B, $38M raised (Sequoia, Reach Capital)
**Employees:** ~120

**What KnowGraph provides:** KnowGraph licenses a "curriculum-aligned content library" to Lumalynx. The library consists of approximately 42,000 math problem sets covering grades 3-12, each tagged with Common Core State Standards (CCSS) alignment codes, difficulty progressions, and multi-step solution explanations. KnowGraph represents that these problem sets are "independently developed by curriculum experts with reference to published standards frameworks."

**Contract terms:**
- Effective date: July 1, 2024
- Term: 3 years (through June 30, 2027) with auto-renewal
- Annual licensing fee: $2.4M (paid quarterly, $600K/quarter)
- Content exclusivity: Non-exclusive (KnowGraph can license to other edtech platforms)
- Termination: 90-day notice for convenience; 30-day notice for material breach with cure period
- Representations & Warranties: KnowGraph represents that all content is "original or properly licensed" and that KnowGraph has "full authority to license the content for the intended use" (see Section 7.2 of the Master License Agreement)
- Indemnification: KnowGraph indemnifies Lumalynx against third-party IP claims arising from the content (see Section 9.1), subject to a liability cap of 2x annual fees ($4.8M)

## CEO Involvement

CEO Raj Venkataraman personally championed the KnowGraph partnership. Key facts:

1. **Personal relationship.** Raj and Anand Mehta (KnowGraph CEO) met at an ASU+GSV conference in April 2023. They maintained a personal relationship through the diligence period. Raj has referred to Anand as "one of the sharpest product minds in edtech" in board meetings.

2. **Board presentation.** Raj presented the KnowGraph partnership to the Lumalynx board in May 2024 as a "strategic content moat" that would differentiate Lumalynx from Khan Academy and Chegg. The board approved the deal unanimously.

3. **LumaSummit announcement.** Raj personally announced the KnowGraph partnership at LumaSummit 2025 (Chicago) in a keynote segment. The announcement included a joint demo with Anand on stage. Video is still on the Lumalynx YouTube channel (14,200 views).

4. **Internal championing.** When the marketing team asked for additional content diligence in Q3 2024, Raj intervened to say the partnership was "vetted at the board level" and that "Anand's team has the curriculum credentials." This effectively short-circuited the independent content audit that Priyanka's team had planned.

5. **Personal investment note.** Per Margo's private observation (not documented): Raj's personal credibility with the board is partially tied to the KnowGraph deal. If the partnership unwinds publicly, it is not just a vendor issue — it reflects on Raj's judgment.

## Exposure Assessment (Preliminary)

If The Verge's allegations are substantiated:

1. **FTC risk:** Every claim referencing "curriculum-aligned" or "publisher-vetted" content may be unsubstantiated. FTC Act Section 5 requires that advertising claims be truthful and substantiated at the time they are made. If KnowGraph misrepresented content provenance to Lumalynx, our defense depends on the reasonableness of our reliance on those representations.

2. **Publisher litigation risk:** If Pearson, McGraw-Hill, or HMH pursue IP claims against KnowGraph, Lumalynx may be named as a co-defendant (downstream distributor of infringing content). The indemnification clause caps KnowGraph's liability at $4.8M, which would not cover a multi-publisher litigation scenario.

3. **Brand risk:** Lumalynx's brand positioning in the K-12 market depends on trust. Parents trust us with their children's education. District buyers trust us with public funds. If we are seen as complicit in content theft — even unknowingly — the brand damage could take 12-18 months to recover.

4. **Revenue risk:** The top-20 ABM district accounts (Project Lighthouse) represent $18.7M in combined contract value. Of these, 14 accounts have active usage of KnowGraph-sourced content. Renewal risk is significant for accounts with renewals in the next 90 days.
