**Classification:** Internal
**Created:** 2024-09-12
**Author:** Fiona Ni Bhreathnach, Director of Brand & PR, EMEA

**Status:** Active (but see note below)

> **Note from Priyanka (2026-03-27):** This playbook was written for a pre-AI-era product crisis (e.g., a data breach or a service outage). It has never been stress-tested against a partner IP crisis or a content provenance issue. Use it as a framework but adapt heavily — do not follow the templates verbatim.

## 1. Crisis Classification

| Level | Definition | Example | Response Time |
|-------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Level 1 — Monitor | Negative coverage with limited reach; no customer impact | A blog post criticizing our pricing | 48-72 hours |
| Level 2 — Respond | Significant coverage or customer impact; contained to one segment | A service outage affecting Home subscribers | 24 hours |
| Level 3 — Mobilize | Major coverage across multiple outlets; multi-segment impact; potential regulatory exposure | A data breach; a partner fraud allegation | 4-6 hours |

**The KnowGraph situation is Level 3.**

## 3. Holding Statement Template

```
[COMPANY NAME] is aware of [DESCRIPTION OF SITUATION]. We take [RELEVANT VALUE — e.g., content integrity, student privacy, data security] seriously and are [CURRENT ACTION — e.g., conducting a thorough review, working with our partners to understand the facts].

We are committed to [RELEVANT COMMITMENT — e.g., maintaining the highest standards for educational content] and will provide an update as our review progresses.

For media inquiries, please contact [PR CONTACT].
```

> **Note from Fiona (2025-01-15):** This template is deliberately bland. In a real crisis, Margo will want the language to reflect Lumalynx's brand voice — which is warmer and more narrative-led than this template. Treat this as a structural skeleton, not final copy.

> **Note from Devika (2024-09-15):** Any holding statement must be reviewed through CLM before external distribution. The template above is a starting point, not pre-cleared language.

## 4. Social Media Response Protocol

**Default posture for Level 3:** Do not respond to social media mentions until a holding statement is approved. Monitor only.

**Monitoring tools:** Sprout Social (scheduling + analytics), Brandwatch (listening), Meltwater (media monitoring)

**Response tiers (once holding statement is approved):**
- **Tier 1 — Ignore:** Trolls, conspiracy theories, competitors amplifying. Do not engage.
- **Tier 2 — Acknowledge:** Sincere customer concerns. Like the post and reply with the holding statement language.
- **Tier 3 — Template reply:** Direct questions from media or influencers. Use the approved template. Do not go off-script.
- **Tier 4 — Escalate:** Anything from a journalist, government official, or large-following account (>50K). Route to Sean immediately.

**Banned phrases in all social media responses (any crisis):**
- "We guarantee..."
- "We can confirm that [content/data/product] is [safe/secure/properly licensed]" (unless legal has confirmed)
- "This is not a big deal" / "This has been blown out of proportion"
- "No comment"
- Any language that names or blames the partner by name in a public-facing context

## 5. Customer Communication Guidelines

**B2C (Home subscribers):**
- Channel: Email (HubSpot)
- Tone: Empathetic, parent-to-parent, acknowledge their concern about their children
- Do NOT promise refunds before Finance/CMO decides
- Do NOT make claims about content status that legal has not cleared

**B2B (District/Classroom buyers):**
- Channel: Account-specific outreach (AE + ABM team)
- Tone: Professional, contract-aware, focused on service continuity and curriculum integrity
- Do NOT make contractual commitments without legal review
- Do NOT use B2C refund language ("we're sorry for the inconvenience" reads differently to a district procurement officer than to a parent)

## 6. Internal Communication

- Stand up a dedicated Slack channel: `#crisis-[topic]`
- All crisis-related discussion happens in the dedicated channel, not in DMs or other channels
- Daily standup at 09:00 ET for the duration of the crisis
- Written daily summary email to CMO at end of day

## 7. Post-Crisis

- 30-day monitoring plan (social sentiment, refund rate, press coverage)
- Internal retrospective within 14 days of crisis resolution
- Playbook update based on lessons learned
- Board briefing (CMO presents)
