## Candidate 1: Cascade Productions

**Company:** Cascade Productions LLC
**Headquarters:** Milwaukee, WI
**Founded:** 2014
**Employees:** ~45
**Estimated Quote:** $180,000 (full AV/production package)

**Overview:**
Cascade is a mid-market AV and event production company that primarily serves the Midwest conference circuit. They have grown steadily since 2014 and have a solid reputation for reliable, no-surprises execution at mid-size events. Their sweet spot is 400-900 attendee conferences with standard AV setups: main stage, breakout rooms, basic lighting, and standard projection.

**Strengths:**
- Competitive pricing — approximately $180K for the full LumaSummit scope, which is roughly half of what StagePro was charging
- Milwaukee-based, within driving distance of Chicago (90 minutes). Reduced travel and logistics costs
- Available for July 14-16 dates (confirmed verbally by their sales director, Mark Hennessy)
- Good track record with on-time delivery and clean load-in/load-out
- Carries $3M general liability insurance (below our $5M recommendation but standard for their size)

**Weaknesses:**
- **Limited interactive technology capability.** Cascade's largest interactive setup was a 3-screen audience polling system for a 600-person real estate conference in 2024. They have not supported live product demos with real-time software interaction, audience-controlled interfaces, or the kind of multi-screen interactive walkthroughs that 6 of our speakers require.
- Maximum event size to date: 850 attendees (Midwest Association of Realtors Annual Conference, 2024). LumaSummit at 1,400 would be their largest event by a significant margin.
- No virtual overflow streaming capability in-house. They would need to subcontract this to a third party (Hopin integration untested by their team).
- Stage design capabilities are functional but not creatively distinctive. Tomasz will likely have concerns.

**References (provided by Cascade):**
1. Midwest EdTech Alliance Annual Conference (Chicago, 2025) — 620 attendees, standard AV. Contact: Diana Kowalski, Executive Director.
2. National Science Teachers Association Regional Meeting (Indianapolis, 2024) — 780 attendees, standard AV + poster session lighting. Contact: Robert Chen, Events Manager.

**Insurance:** $3M general liability, $1M professional liability, $500K equipment floater.

## Candidate 2: Meridian Live

**Company:** Meridian Live Productions Inc.
**Headquarters:** New York, NY
**Founded:** 2008
**Employees:** ~180
**Estimated Quote:** $420,000 (full AV/production package)

**Overview:**
Meridian Live is a top-tier event production company with a client roster that includes Salesforce, Google, Microsoft, and several Fortune 100 companies. They are the premium option — their production quality is outstanding, their tech capabilities are state-of-the-art, and they have managed events with 5,000+ attendees. They are significantly more expensive than the other candidates.

**Strengths:**
- Elite production quality. Their portfolio includes Salesforce Dreamforce satellite events, Google I/O regional stages, and Microsoft Ignite breakout halls.
- Full interactive technology stack: multi-screen interactive demos, real-time audience participation systems, AR/VR stage elements, live software walkthroughs with audience-controlled interfaces. Every one of our 6 AV-dependent speakers would be fully supported.
- Proven at scale: events from 500 to 12,000 attendees. LumaSummit at 1,400 is well within their comfort zone.
- In-house virtual overflow streaming with native Hopin integration (they have done this for 3 previous clients).
- Excellent insurance: $10M general liability, $5M professional liability, $2M equipment floater.
- Creative stage design team that has won multiple Event Marketer EX Awards.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Price.** At $420K, Meridian is the most expensive option. Combined with the $340K StagePro write-off, the total AV/production line item would be approximately $760K — roughly $250K over the original StagePro budget. This puts total event spend at approximately $1.48M, which exceeds Margo's $1.4M all-in hard cap.
- NYC-based. Travel, freight, and per diem costs for a Chicago event will add approximately $25-35K on top of the quote.
- Availability for July 14-16 is "likely but not confirmed" — they have a tentative hold for a healthcare conference in Boston that same week. Final answer expected by March 25.
- They are accustomed to higher-budget clients and their account management style can feel transactional to smaller organizations.

**References (provided by Meridian):**
1. Salesforce Dreamforce Satellite Event (Chicago, 2025) — 2,200 attendees, full interactive production. Contact: Jennifer Montoya, Salesforce Events.
2. EdSurge Innovation Summit (San Francisco, 2024) — 900 attendees, interactive demo stage. Contact: Prashant Ravi, EdSurge.
3. Google for Education APAC Regional (Singapore, 2025) — 1,100 attendees, multi-language simultaneous streaming. Contact: Wei Lin Tan, Google Events.

**Insurance:** $10M general liability, $5M professional liability, $2M equipment floater.

## Candidate 3: Vanguard Events

**Company:** Vanguard Events Group LLC
**Headquarters:** Chicago, IL
**Founded:** 2011
**Employees:** ~90
**Estimated Quote:** $290,000 (full AV/production package)

**Overview:**
Vanguard is a Chicago-based event production company with strong capabilities in the education and nonprofit conference space. They are the mid-range option — solid production quality, good interactive tech, and the significant advantage of being local to the venue (McCormick Place West). They have managed events for several education organizations and their technical team has experience with live demo environments.

**Strengths:**
- **Chicago-based.** McCormick Place is their home venue — they have loaded in and out of the West building at least 8 times in the past 3 years. Local logistics advantage is significant: no freight costs, local crew relationships, faster site visits, and a McCormick Place union crew familiarity that out-of-town vendors lack.
- Strong interactive technology capabilities: multi-screen setups, live demo environments, audience polling and participation systems. Not at Meridian's level but significantly above Cascade.
- Solid education conference experience: National School Boards Association (NSBA) Annual Conference (2024, 3,200 attendees), ISTE Annual Conference support stage (2023, 1,800 attendees), and several state-level education conferences.
- In-house virtual overflow streaming capability with Hopin integration (tested and deployed twice).
- Competitive price at $290K — within budget even with the full $340K write-off (total approximately $1.35M, under Margo's $1.4M cap).
- Available and confirmed for July 14-16 dates.
- Insurance: $5M general liability, $2M professional liability, $1M equipment floater.

**Weaknesses:**
- **POTENTIAL CONFLICT OF INTEREST.** Unconfirmed reports indicate that Vanguard Events is currently contracted with Khan Academy for their annual Khan Academy Summit scheduled for September 2026. If confirmed, this creates a competitive conflict: Vanguard's production team would have access to Lumalynx's conference content, product launch plans, stage design, and proprietary speaker materials while simultaneously working with our primary K-12 competitor. This must be verified during reference checks.
- Creative stage design is competent but not distinctive — their portfolio shows functional, clean designs without the creative ambition Tomasz would prefer.
- Largest single event: 3,200 attendees (NSBA), but that was a co-production with another vendor. Largest solo production: 1,800 attendees.
- Account management team is stretched — they recently lost their senior project manager and the replacement is 4 months into the role.

**References (provided by Vanguard):**
1. National School Boards Association Annual Conference (New Orleans, 2024) — 3,200 attendees (co-production). Contact: Maria Santos, NSBA Events.
2. Illinois State Board of Education Annual Meeting (Chicago, 2025) — 650 attendees, interactive tech. Contact: James Okafor, ISBE.
3. American Association of School Administrators Leadership Conference (Nashville, 2024) — 1,100 attendees. Contact: Patricia Hernandez, AASA.

**Insurance:** $5M general liability, $2M professional liability, $1M equipment floater.
